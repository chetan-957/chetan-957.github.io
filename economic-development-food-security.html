The Interplay Between Economic Development, Environmental Factors & Food Security

Project Overview

This project analyzes how economic development and environmental factors interact with food security outcomes across countries over time. Using multiple real-world datasets, the analysis explores relationships between income levels, emissions, and food availability indicators.

Datasets Used

  • FAOSTAT Food Balance Sheets (food supply & nutrition indicators)
  • World Bank World Development Indicators (GDP, GNI, inequality)
  • Environmental datasets (CO₂ and related emissions indicators)

Data Cleaning & Integration

Multiple datasets were cleaned, standardized, and merged at the country–year level. This included handling missing values, harmonizing country names, aligning time ranges, and creating derived indicators for analysis.

Analysis & Visualization

Exploratory data analysis and visualization techniques were applied to examine trends, correlations, and cross-country differences. Visualizations were used to highlight relationships between economic growth, environmental pressure, and food security outcomes.

Key Insights

  • Economic development is strongly associated with improved food availability, but gains vary across regions.
  • Environmental pressures such as emissions show complex relationships with food security indicators.
  • Higher-income countries exhibit different food security and sustainability trade-offs compared to lower-income countries.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how integrating economic, environmental, and food system data can provide a more comprehensive understanding of global food security challenges and trade-offs.
