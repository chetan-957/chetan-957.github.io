The Interplay Between Economic Development, Environmental Factors & Food Security

Project Overview

This project examines how economic development and environmental factors interact to shape food security outcomes across countries over time. By integrating multiple real-world datasets, the analysis explores whether economic growth consistently translates into improved food availability, and how environmental pressures complicate this relationship.

Datasets Used

  • FAOSTAT Food Balance Sheets (food supply & nutrition indicators)
  • World Bank World Development Indicators (GDP, GNI, inequality)
  • Environmental datasets (CO₂ and related emissions indicators)

Data Cleaning & Integration

The analysis required extensive preprocessing, including harmonizing country identifiers, aligning time ranges across datasets, handling missing values, and creating derived indicators at the country–year level. Multiple datasets were merged into a unified analytical structure to support cross-country and temporal comparisons.

Analysis & Visualization

Exploratory data analysis and visualization techniques were applied to examine trends, correlations, and distributional differences across income groups and regions. Visualizations were used to compare food availability metrics against economic and environmental indicators, highlighting structural patterns rather than isolated observations.

Key Insights

  • Economic development is associated with higher food availability, but the magnitude of improvement varies significantly across income groups and regions.
  • Upper-middle-income countries showed stronger marginal gains in food availability compared to high-income countries, where improvements tended to plateau.
  • Environmental indicators such as emissions reveal trade-offs, suggesting that improvements in food security are often accompanied by increased environmental pressure.
  • High-income countries exhibit distinct food security and sustainability patterns, highlighting that economic growth alone does not guarantee environmentally balanced outcomes.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how combining economic, environmental, and food system data provides a more nuanced understanding of global food security challenges. The findings highlight the importance of evaluating development outcomes through both social and environmental lenses when assessing long-term sustainability.
